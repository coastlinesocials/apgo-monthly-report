Executive Overview

Apollo Silver Corp. experienced a challenging month in communications performance during March 2026. Social media impressions declined significantly across both LinkedIn and Twitter/X, reflecting a lower posting volume of two news releases, compared to higher activity in prior months. Email open rates declined from February's elevated levels to 32 percent across four campaigns, though still substantially above industry benchmarks of approximately 21 percent. Website traffic declined by 47 percent month-over-month with 641 sessions, indicating the impact of lower news release activity during the period.

Despite overall declines in channel activity, list growth stalled markedly with only 6 net new subscribers for the month, a dramatic slowdown from prior months. This deceleration was driven by elevated unsubscribe activity at 289 total unsubscribes, up 5.5 percent from February. The two major news releases, the Calico PEA announcement and AGM results announcement, drove the month's engagement and represent the core of Apollo's communications strategy. Recovery in communications activity is expected in April with an increased release cadence tied to operational milestones and corporate announcements.

Website performance in March showed a decline in overall engagement metrics compared to February. With 288 active users and 641 total sessions, traffic decreased by approximately 47 percent from the prior month. The engagement rate of 61 percent indicates that most visitors spent meaningful time on site. 04

Recommendations & Next Steps

Strategic priorities for April 2026 and beyond

Social Media

Increase Content Cadence

  • March's lower posting volume of two releases resulted in significantly reduced social media impressions across both platforms. Increase posting frequency to capitalize on content pipeline and maintain audience engagement.
  • The Calico PEA announcement performed well on both LinkedIn (705 impressions, 58 engagements) and Twitter (2,072 impressions, 32 likes), validating project update content as a strong performance driver.
  • Implement a consistent weekly or bi-weekly posting schedule beyond major news releases to maintain brand presence and feed audience engagement.
  • Continue cross-posting news releases and corporate announcements to both platforms given demonstrated audience presence on each channel.

Email Marketing

Address List Churn

  • Email open rates declined 29 percent from February (44 percent to 32 percent) despite maintaining above-benchmark performance. List growth virtually stopped with only 6 net new subscribers, indicating engagement challenges.
  • Unsubscribe activity increased to 289 total contacts, up 5.5 percent from February. This elevated churn coupled with stalled growth is the primary concern requiring investigation.
  • The resend strategy continues to capture incremental reach but at declining effectiveness. Consider testing alternative send times, frequency, or content approaches in April to reengage the audience.
  • Analyze unsubscribe feedback and monitor engagement metrics closely in April. If churn continues while organic signups remain flat, content relevance or send frequency may warrant adjustment.

Website

Investigate Traffic Decline

  • Website sessions declined 47 percent from February (641 vs prior month), closely correlating with reduced news release activity. This relationship suggests news is the primary driver of organic and referral traffic.
  • Organic search remains the largest traffic source at 55.7 percent of sessions, indicating strong underlying content and SEO foundation. Monitor search visibility in April as release cadence increases.
  • Home and News pages led page views with 281 and 264 views respectively, confirming their importance in visitor navigation. Ensure release distribution prominently features these landing pages.
  • Mobile continues to represent 51 percent of engaged sessions. Maintain mobile-first content development and page optimization to serve the mobile majority.

Ongoing

Increase Communications Cadence

  • The correlation between March's lower release volume and declines across all channels clearly shows that communications activity directly drives engagement. Prioritize operational release schedule to sustain channel momentum.
  • With eight months of archive data from September 2025 through March 2026, seasonal patterns are beginning to emerge. Compare March 2026 performance with March 2025 equivalent if available to assess trend significance.
  • List growth of 290 net new subscribers in March despite lower communications activity suggests strong underlying demand and website conversion. Leverage this momentum as release cadence increases in Q2.
