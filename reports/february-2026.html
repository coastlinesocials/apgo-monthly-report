Executive Overview

Apollo Silver's communications activity in February delivered some of the strongest results recorded across all channels since reporting began. Two high-impact announcements drove the month's performance: the Company's acceptance into the U.S. Defense Industrial Base Consortium and its recognition as 13th overall in the 2026 TSX Venture 50, both of which resonated strongly with investors across social media, email, and the website. The February results confirm that milestone-driven communications continue to be Apollo's most powerful engagement tool, with content tied to corporate achievements and external recognition outperforming all other content types by a wide margin.

LinkedIn delivered an exceptional month, with impressions increasing 242 percent and clicks rising 424 percent month over month, reflecting strong investor interest in Apollo's growing profile and strategic positioning. Twitter/X also continued its upward trend, with impressions up 52 percent and likes more than doubling. Email performance remained well above industry benchmarks, with open rates across all four primary campaigns ranging from 43 to 49 percent. Mailing list growth accelerated further, adding 78 new contacts entirely through organic website signups. Website traffic improved materially, with active users rising to 461 and engagement quality remaining strong, with 444 users spending more than 30 seconds on site and an average session duration of over three minutes.

Recommendations & Next Steps

Strategic priorities for March 2026 and beyond

Social Media

Build on Momentum

  • Continue prioritizing milestone-driven content as the TSX Venture 50 recognition and board announcements delivered the strongest engagement of any month to date.
  • Maintain or increase posting frequency on LinkedIn to capitalize on the 242 percent impression growth achieved in February.
  • Expand video content usage across both platforms given that video posts featured among the top performing content in February on both LinkedIn and Twitter/X.
  • Continue cross-posting third-party recognition and external coverage to reinforce Apollo's growing profile and credibility with investors.

Mailouts

Sustain & Refine

  • Maintain the resend strategy to non-openers as it continues to capture meaningful additional reach without duplicating sends to engaged contacts.
  • Continue monitoring open rates against the industry benchmark; February's performance of 43 to 49 percent remains well above sector norms.
  • Refine click-through calls to action on news release campaigns, which consistently show lower click rates than CEO blog and leadership content.
  • With 78 new organic signups in February, continue reinforcing website signup form visibility to sustain list growth momentum.

Website

Deepen Engagement

  • The DIBC page entering the top pages list confirms that news-driven traffic spills directly onto the website; continue deep-linking news releases to relevant project and corporate pages.
  • Prioritize SEO improvements on the Calico and Cinco project pages, which continue to draw consistent traffic and represent Apollo's core investor content.
  • With mobile now accounting for 52.5 percent of engaged sessions, review mobile layout and load speed across key investor pages to ensure the experience matches desktop quality.

Ongoing

Track & Report

  • Maintain this monthly reporting framework to track performance and identify trends across all communications channels.
  • With six months of data now available from September 2025 through February 2026, begin identifying seasonal patterns and benchmarking months against their prior year equivalents as the archive grows.
  • Continue strengthening UTM tracking across social posts and mailouts to more precisely attribute website sessions to specific campaigns.
