Executive Overview

Apollo Silver's communications activity in November focused on maintaining visibility across social media, email, and web channels following a quieter news cycle relative to October's financing-driven momentum. Engagement quality across platforms remained solid; however, overall audience growth and inbound website traffic did not show the expected lift despite multiple marketing initiatives, suggesting that existing outreach programs are successfully retaining attention but not meaningfully expanding the Company's reach.

Overall, November presented a clear and consistent picture: Apollo's core audience remains engaged and responsive, but the Company is not yet seeing measurable expansion of its digital footprint. With GA4 fully implemented and enhanced SEO, a structured social media rollout planned for January 2026, and stronger cross-promotion around upcoming events and conferences, Apollo is well positioned to drive improved performance and broader investor reach heading into the new year.

Notable Observation

Website traffic remained flat or declined across every major acquisition channel despite sustained posting activity, news releases, and the engagement of new third-party marketing groups. While Apollo's existing audience is engaged, the Company is not currently attracting a larger volume of new investors to the website. SEO improvements across project and investor pages will be an important priority heading into 2026 to expand organic discoverability and broaden reach.

04

Notable Observations

Key patterns and strategic considerations from November

Website traffic remains stagnant despite increased marketing input

Current outreach programs are maintaining visibility but not effectively expanding Apollo's audience base. Total users declined month-over-month and no meaningful lift materialized from social media, news releases, or third-party marketing activity.

Internal content continues to outperform external marketing efforts

News Releases consistently deliver the highest engagement across social media, email, and website channels. Internally produced, project-focused content remains Apollo's strongest communications asset, outperforming paid or external marketing initiatives.

SEO limitations may be reducing organic discovery

Compared with peer companies, Apollo's organic search visibility appears constrained, likely due to under-optimized SEO across key pages and news releases. Improving SEO for project pages, leadership profiles, and future news releases will be important to expand visibility and broaden reach.

Engagement quality is strong, but audience size is not growing

Users who do visit the website remain engaged for meaningful durations, indicating content relevance. However, the absence of growth in total users implies that Apollo's communications are primarily reaching existing followers rather than attracting new ones.

05

Recommendations & Next Steps

Strategic priorities for December 2025 and the 2026 rollout

Social Media

Prepare for 2026 Rollout

  • Finalize the updated social media strategy in December and prepare for a clean January 2026 rollout rather than launching briefly before the holiday slowdown.
  • Beginning in January, increase structured posting frequency across LinkedIn and Twitter/X to enhance reach and drive higher-quality referral traffic.
  • Prioritize milestone-driven content including financings, project advancements, technical insights, and leadership commentary, as these consistently generate the strongest investor engagement.

Mailouts

Optimize & Segment

  • Maintain timely, news-driven campaigns as open rates continue to outperform industry averages by a wide margin.
  • Begin implementing A/B testing on subject line tone, CTA placement, and button versus hyperlink formats to identify which combinations best convert engagement into website traffic.
  • Review list segmentation in the new year to ensure investors, media, and industry contacts receive appropriately tailored messaging.

Website

SEO & Discoverability

  • Establish November and December as baseline months to measure the impact of 2026 marketing activity with greater accuracy.
  • Prioritize SEO improvements across project and investor pages to address stagnant traffic from new audiences.
  • Expand deep-linking and consistent UTM tracking in social posts and emails to more precisely measure which channels are driving qualified sessions.

Ongoing

Measure & Reassess

  • Reassess all acquisition channels in January and February to determine whether the new 2026 social media plan and increased posting cadence produce measurable improvements.
  • Begin reviewing and strengthening SEO throughout Q1 2026, focusing on project pages, leadership pages, and News/Investors sections.
  • Continue building out internal communications systems to support a more cohesive and targeted investor relations strategy moving forward.
